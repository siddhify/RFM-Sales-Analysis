## Understanding Customer Behavior to Drive Better Decisions  

### Overview  
A company provided 12 months of sales data, and this project analyzes customer behavior to support targeted marketing and better customer engagement. 
The goal is to understand which customers are most valuable, which are at risk, and how the business can improve retention and communication strategies.

### Approach  
Customer activity across 12 months was combined to create a complete view of engagement.  

Each customer was evaluated based on:  
- How recently they made a purchase  
- How frequently they purchase  
- How much they contribute in revenue  

These patterns were used to group customers into distinct segments based on their level of engagement and value to the business.

### Key Customer Segments  
The analysis revealed clear customer groups:  

- High-value, loyal customers who consistently contribute to revenue  
- Active customers with potential to increase their value  
- Customers showing early signs of disengagement  
- Inactive or lost customers who have dropped off over time  

---

### Business Relevance  
This project moves beyond reporting numbers and focuses on decision-making.  

It helps answer critical questions such as:  
- Where should the business focus its retention efforts?  
- Which customers are at risk of being lost without intervention?  
- How can marketing efforts be more targeted and effective?  

By identifying these patterns early, businesses can avoid silent customer loss and make more efficient use of resources.

---

### Potential Actions  
The insights from this analysis can directly support:  

- Strengthening relationships with high-value customers  
- Re-engaging at-risk customers before they churn  
- Designing targeted campaigns instead of broad messaging  
- Prioritizing efforts that improve long-term customer value  

---

### Outcome  
The final output includes a structured customer dataset and an interactive dashboard that provides a clear view of customer behavior.  
This enables stakeholders to quickly understand where value lies and where action is needed.

---

### Key Takeaway  
Customer behavior is not uniform, and business decisions should not be either.  

Segmenting customers based on their actual engagement allows for more focused strategies, stronger retention, and more sustainable growth.
